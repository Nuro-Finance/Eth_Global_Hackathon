07 · On-chain

On-chain infrastructure — 23 chains

13 chains support native-token auto-swap (Marathon 7): Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, BSC, Avalanche, Linea, Scroll, Unichain, World Chain, Sonic, HyperEVM. 23 chains total accept USDC deposits — add zkSync, Celo, Gnosis, Sei, XDC, Codex, Ink, Plume, Monad, and Solana (via Circle Bridge Kit). Every route ends at the user's Issuer-linked Base address. Below: the routing decision, the actual contracts involved, and a worked example of the most complex path (LZ hop).

03 — Worked example: HyperEVM native path (most complex)

Deposits on HyperEVM take the longest route: native swap → LZ hop to Arbitrum → CCTP burn to Base. Here's what that looks like end-to-end.

Scenario — User 42 sends 5 HYPE from their wallet to deposit address 0xDEP…42A1
1

HYPE lands at deposit address

Wallet signs a native transfer. HyperEVM confirms in ~2s.

chain: HyperEVM · tx hash pattern: 0xhy…pe1
2

Monitor poll detects native balance

Balance exceeds GAS_BUFFER_BY_CHAIN['hyperevm'] = 0.002 HYPE → swap-eligible. Backend fetches fresh quote from 0x for HyperEVM.

3

0x swap executes → USDC lands at same address

Deployer signs:

0xAllowanceHolderHyperEVM.swap(HYPE → USDC, 5e18, recipient=0xDEP…42A1)

~$10 USDC arrives. swapInflight TTL expires, next poll tick sees USDC.

4

Bridge decision → HyperEVM not CCTP-enabled → LZ path

Backend calls:

USDC.approve(OFTAdapter, amount) OFTAdapter.send(SendParam{dstEid: 30110, to: 0xd58C…2b18, amount}, fee, refund=0xDEP…42A1)
dstEid 30110 = Arbitrum · arrival time: ~3–5 min
5

USDC lands at Arbitrum hub → CCTP burn to Base

Reconciler detects hub balance increase attributed to this correlation_id. Backend calls:

TokenMessenger.depositForBurn(amount, destDomain=6, mintRecipient=userIssuerAddr, burnToken=USDC)
6

Circle attestation → Base mint → card credit

10–15 min for Circle to attest, then messageTransmitter.receiveMessage(msg, sig) on Base mints USDC at the Issuer-linked address. SD3 webhook fires, card balance updated.

total user-perceived time from signing the HYPE transfer: ~20–30 min
04 — Gas buffers & edge cases

Gas buffers are per-chain (not binary)

The Arbitrum gas incident (Session 23) taught us that a binary chainId === 1 ? mainnet : other split misses real behavior. Now GAS_BUFFER_BY_CHAIN in monitor.ts has explicit entries per chain:

  • ETH mainnet — 0.001 ETH
  • Polygon — 0.5 MATIC
  • BSC — 0.002 BNB
  • L2s (Base, Op, Scroll, Linea, Unichain, World) — 0.0002 ETH
  • Arbitrum — 0.003 ETH (bumped after the Session 23 incident)
  • Avalanche — 0.05 AVAX
  • Sonic — 0.5 S
  • HyperEVM — 0.002 HYPE

Why 13 chains for native swap, not all 23

0x Aggregator's v2 allowance-holder endpoint doesn't have routes on every chain we support. We verified each chain live during Session 23 (commit b8cb173). Confirmed supported: the 13 listed above. Confirmed unsupported: zkSync, Celo, Gnosis (0x returned 'Invalid chain ID'). Too new for routing: Sei, XDC, Codex, Ink, Plume, Monad.

Chains without 0x routes still accept USDC deposits — they route through CCTP or LayerZero to Base. Native token deposits on those chains are un-swappable and sit in the user's deposit address.

23-chain E2E test matrix

Live bridge testing confirmed 6/23 as of Session 19 (Base, Ethereum, Arbitrum, Solana, zkSync, HyperEVM). Richard's expected funding arrival unblocks the remaining 17 across Q2. Each chain needs: (a) native-token swap dry-run, (b) $1 USDC round-trip to Base, (c) card-credit confirmation.
